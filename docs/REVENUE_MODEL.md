# Bolvi Care Revenue Model

## Overview

Bolvi Care LLC is a **licensed Washington home care agency** based in Everett, WA, serving the King–Snohomish County corridor. We bill families directly for care delivered by **Care Partners we employ as W-2 employees**. This is an agency direct-billing model — not a marketplace, and not a fee on top of independent-contractor transactions.

Revenue comes from three sources: hourly care billing (primary), a Premium Family Membership, and adjacent referral relationships.

---

## Care Rates

Bolvi bills families by the hour across three rate tiers:

| Rate tier | Rate | Description |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Family-managed standard | $45/hr | Standard scheduled in-home care arranged and managed by family |
| Urgent / same-day | $55/hr | Same-day and short-notice care requests |
| Self-pay senior | $39/hr | A distinct, lower tier addressing documented senior price sensitivity |

The self-pay senior rate is a deliberate access decision: a lower price point, and correspondingly the thinnest margin of the three tiers, aimed at seniors paying out of pocket.

Same-day / urgent care is a staffed **service capability** priced at the urgent rate — the agency schedules and staffs to respond quickly.

---

## Premium Family Membership — $35/mo

Membership is a **service-level** subscription, not a discount. Members pay the same hourly rates above; what the fee buys is responsiveness and coordination:

- Priority scheduling
- Guaranteed same-day response
- 24-hour replacement guarantee
- Dedicated care coordinator
- Shared family care timeline

There is **no hourly discount** for members. The value is service level, not price.

---

## Care Partner Compensation & Fully-Loaded Cost

Care Partners are W-2 employees. Base wages:

| Role | Base wage |
|------|-----------|
| Home Care Aide (HCA) | $23/hr |
| CNA / EMT | $27/hr |
| **Blended base (approx.)** | **~$25/hr** |

On top of base wages, Bolvi carries the full employer cost of a W-2 workforce. The **fully-loaded blended cost is approximately $32.17/hr**, comprising:

- **Employer payroll taxes (~10.69%)** — FICA (7.65%), FUTA, WA SUTA, and WA Paid Family & Medical Leave (PFML)
- **L&I workers' compensation (3.5%)**
- **Paid sick leave**
- **QSEHRA health-reimbursement stipend**
- **Training**
- **Background checks**
- **Cancellation reserve**
- **Payroll and scheduling administration**

These are real, recurring costs of employing caregivers directly rather than contracting them — and they are the reason the model runs at a lower gross margin than franchise peers.

---

## Gross Margin & Margin Discipline

At the standard family-managed rate, the per-hour bridge is:

```
Family-managed standard rate:      $45.00 / hr
Fully-loaded blended Care Partner:  $32.17 / hr
Gross contribution:                 $12.83 / hr  (~29% gross margin)
```

Bolvi operates at a **gross margin of roughly 29–30%** on standard care — **deliberately below the 50–60% gross margin typical of franchise home-care brands** (e.g., Home Instead, Visiting Angels).

This is a chosen tradeoff, not a weakness. The margin difference is redirected to the people delivering care: Care Partners are paid more and carry full W-2 benefits and protections. The urgent tier ($55/hr) carries a wider margin; the self-pay senior tier ($39/hr) is intentionally the thinnest, trading margin for access. Across the mix, the model is priced to fund a stable, well-paid workforce rather than to maximize per-hour extraction.

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Care billing (primary)

Hourly billing for care delivered, across the three rate tiers. This is the core of the business.

### 2. Premium Family Membership

Recurring $35/mo service-level subscription (see above). Predictable recurring revenue tied to service quality, not to discounting care.

### 3. Adjacent referrals

Referral relationships for services families need alongside care:

- Durable medical equipment (DME)
- Transportation
- Supplies

## Billing & Payroll Flow

Bolvi is the billing party and the employer of record.

```
Care delivered
  └─> Bolvi bills the family at the applicable hourly rate
        (family-managed standard, urgent/same-day, or self-pay senior)

Care Partner paid as a W-2 employee
  └─> Wages paid through payroll
  └─> Employer taxes withheld and remitted (FICA, FUTA, WA SUTA, WA PFML)
  └─> L&I workers' compensation covered
  └─> Paid sick leave accrued; QSEHRA stipend applied
```

Families are billed by the agency for care delivered; Care Partners are paid as employees through payroll. There is no per-transaction service fee and no caregiver payout/withdrawal mechanism — Care Partners are staff, not contractors.

**Payroll is handled externally.** W-2 payroll, tax withholding, and year-end W-2 issuance run through an external payroll provider. The application does not implement in-app payouts, 1099 generation, or contractor tax filing.

---

## Positioning

- **Compliance as a moat.** Correct W-2 classification (satisfying Washington's ABC test) removes the misclassification exposure carried by gig / marketplace platforms.
- **Retention drives quality.** Better pay and W-2 stability keep Care Partners longer, which keeps the same trusted caregivers in front of families — the primary driver of care quality.
- **Margin discipline is intentional.** Running below the franchise-standard margin is how the model funds continuity and retention rather than a sign of weak economics.
